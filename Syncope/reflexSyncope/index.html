Knowns & Unknowns of CardioNeural Ablation
Knowns
  • Not for vasodepressor type.
  • Not for patients with intrinsic SA or AV node dysfunction.
  • Complications included injury to phrenic nerve, coronary artery, and other complications similar to SVT ablation
  • Reflex syncope has a benign prognosis.
Unknowns
  • Long-term effects of re-innervation and high heart rate.
  • RA vs. Bi-atrial Approach
  • Endpoint???
  • Therapeutic Fashion vs. Evidence-based Treatment

#6 Avoid Triggers
CONCLUSIONS
  • Reflex Syncope is a benign condition.
  • Current strategy to prevent reflex syncope include medications, pacemaker, exercise maneuvers, and etc.
    None of the above are highly effective.
  • Cardioneural ablation is a new and promising therapeutic option. Long-term effects and side effects have yet to be established.

AI in Cardiology Examples

AutoEF: Achieves comparable accuracy to human measurements with less variance in repeat testing.

AI in Stress Echo: The PROTEUS trial suggests AI benefits low-volume centers for severe CAD predictions.

References: Nature 2020;580(7802):252-256, NEJM AI 2024;1(11)

AI in Mitral Regurgitation

AI quantifies MR with 80% accuracy for six-degree classifications and 99% accuracy for +/-1 degree.

References: Circulation 2024 Sep;150(12):911-933

AI in Coronary Angiography

AI-QCA: Randomized trials show similar outcomes to human interpretation with better consistency in stent evaluations.

AI-FFR: Randomized trials demonstrate cost reductions and less radiation compared to invasive FFR.

References: J Am Coll Cardiol Intv 2024, PROVISION Study TCT 2024

AI in ECG Interpretation

AI identifies asymptomatic LV systolic dysfunction with an AUC of 0.93 and can predict hypertension with high accuracy.

References: Nat Med 2019;25:70-74, Eur Heart J Digit Health 2024;5(3):247-259

AI Challenges

  • Alert fatigue and indecisiveness
  • Bias and misinformation
  • Loss of human touch
  • AI hallucination and model collapse

Do's and Don'ts of AI in Cardiology

Do's:

  • Ensure quality and security of data
  • Use AI to augment, not replace
  • Encourage explainable AI
  • Stay updated and educated

Don'ts:

  • Don’t rely solely on AI
  • Don’t lose clinical senses
  • Avoid “black-box” models for critical diagnoses
  • Don’t overhype AI results to patients
